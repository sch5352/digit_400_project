Avians of the Antarctic

Of the 17 penguin species, only six of them reside in the Antarctic. On this page you will find information on each of the six species. Because these penguins reside in the Southern Hemisphere, they are declining in population due to climate change and overfishing. We hope that by familiarizing yourselves with these creatures, you will gain more respect and a better understanding for them and the environment we live in.


Adelie Penguin
  • Scientific Name- Pygoscelis adeliae
  • Diet- Main food source is krill and small fish but occasionally feed on jellyfish
  • Size- 27.5 inches *The Adelie Penguin is the smallest species of penguin in the Antarctic
  • Weight- 8.5 - 12lbs
  • Speed- Waddle around 1.5 mph but can swim approximately 2.5 to 5 mph and reach speeds up to 9.3 mph
  • Remaining Population- ~4.5 million breeding pairs
    • Chinstrap Penguin
  • Scientific Name- Pygoscelis Antarcticus
  • Diet- Feed on krill, small fish, and shrimp
  • Size- 24 - 27 inches
  • Weight- 6.6 - 13lbs
  • Speed- Usually "toboggan" on land on their stomachs but swim up to 20 mph
  • Remaining Population- ~7.5 million breeding pairs
    • Emperor Penguin
  • Scientific Name- Aptenodytes forsteri
  • Diet- Feed on krill and small fish along with squid and crustaceans
  • Size- 45 inches or 3.6 - 4.5 feet *Emperor Penguins are the largest of all living penguin species
  • Weight- up to 88lbs
  • Speed- Can swim up to 15 mph
  • Remaining Population- ~200,000 breeding pairs
    • Gentoo Penguin
  • Scientific Name- Pygoscelis papua
  • Diet- Feed on krill, small fish, shrimp, along with squid and crustaceans
  • Size- 20 - 36 inches
  • Weight- 8.8 - 18lbs
  • Speed- Can reach swimming speeds of up to 30mph *Gentoo Penguins are the fastest species of penguin
  • Remaining Population- ~300,000 breeding pairs
    • King Penguin
  • Scientific Name- Aptenodytes Patagonicus
  • Diet- Feed mainly on krill, small fish, and shrimp
  • Size- 24 - 35 inches *King Penguins are the second largest species of penguin
  • Weight- 21 - 38lbs
  • Speed- Swim 4 - 6mph
  • Remaining Population- ~2 million breeding pairs
    • Macaroni Penguin
  • Scientific Name- Eudyptes Chrysolophus
  • Diet- Feed mainly on fish, squid, and crustaceans
  • Size- 24 - 28 inches
  • Weight- 7 - 14lbs
  • Speed- Reach speeds up to 22mph
  • Remaining Population- ~6.3 million breeding pairs
